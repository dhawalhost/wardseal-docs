# Policy: Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** 15 March 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Wardseal ("WardSeal", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects personal data when providing the WardSeal identity and access platform.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to:

- Public website and marketing pages
- WardSeal cloud services (including admin console and APIs)
- Customer support and account management interactions

For self-hosted deployments, customers control hosting and infrastructure choices. This policy still applies to interactions with us directly (for example, support, billing, and account administration).

## 2. Roles

Depending on context, we may act as:

- **Controller** for account, billing, and business contact data
- **Processor** for customer-managed end-user identity data processed on behalf of customers

## 3. Data we collect

### 3.1 Data customers provide

- Account profile information (name, work email, company)
- Contract and billing details
- Support requests and communications

### 3.2 Data processed in the service

WardSeal may process identity and authentication-related data, such as:

- User identifiers (for example email, username, user ID)
- Organization and tenant context
- Authentication events (login attempts, MFA events, token issuance events)
- Device and security telemetry used to secure authentication sessions
- Audit log metadata generated by administrative operations

### 3.3 Automatically collected data

- Security and diagnostics logs
- Service usage metrics
- Technical metadata needed for availability, abuse prevention, and troubleshooting

## 4. How we use data

We use personal data to:

- Provide, secure, and improve WardSeal services
- Authenticate users and enforce access controls
- Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access
- Provide customer support
- Comply with legal obligations

## 5. Legal bases (EEA/UK)

Where GDPR or UK GDPR applies, we rely on one or more legal bases:

- Performance of a contract
- Legitimate interests (for security, fraud prevention, and service improvement)
- Legal obligation
- Consent (where required)

### India (DPDP Act, 2023)

Where India data protection law applies, WardSeal processes personal data for lawful purposes related to service delivery, security, fraud prevention, compliance, and customer-requested operations.

Where consent is the basis, consent is requested through appropriate notices and can be withdrawn subject to legal and contractual limitations.

### United States (state privacy law baseline)

Where applicable U.S. state privacy laws apply, we provide required notices and rights workflows, including access, correction, deletion, and portability, subject to statutory exceptions.

## 6. Sharing and disclosure

We may share data with:

- Service providers/subprocessors that help operate the service
- Professional advisors (for example legal, audit)
- Authorities when legally required
- Parties involved in a corporate transaction (subject to safeguards)

We do not sell personal data in exchange for monetary compensation.

## 7. International transfers

If data is transferred across borders, we use appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent lawful mechanisms.

## 8. Data retention

We retain personal data for as long as needed to provide the service, meet contractual obligations, resolve disputes, and comply with law. Detailed retention periods are documented in `data-retention-and-deletion-policy.md`.

## 9. Security measures

We use technical and organizational measures designed to protect data, including access controls, encryption in transit, tenant isolation controls, and audit logging. Additional details are in `security-and-trust.md`.

## 10. Your rights

Depending on location, individuals may have rights to:

- Access personal data
- Correct inaccurate data
- Delete data
- Restrict or object to processing
- Data portability
- Withdraw consent (when consent is the legal basis)

To submit requests, contact: privacy@wardseal.com.

### 10.1 EU/EEA rights (GDPR)

Individuals may have rights to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability, and complaint to a supervisory authority.

### 10.2 India rights (DPDP)

Data principals may have rights to access information about processing, correction and erasure, grievance redressal, and nomination rights, as applicable under law.

### 10.3 U.S. state privacy rights

Residents of certain U.S. states may have rights to know/access, correct, delete, and obtain copies of personal data, plus rights related to certain profiling and targeted advertising contexts where applicable.

WardSeal does not sell personal data for monetary consideration. If your implementation uses sharing for targeted advertising, update this policy and provide required opt-out mechanisms.

## 10.4 Verification and appeals

We may verify request identity before fulfilling rights requests. Where required by law, we provide a process to appeal request decisions.

## 11. Cookies and tracking

Our use of cookies and similar technologies is described in `cookie-policy.md`.

## 12. Children

WardSeal is intended for business and enterprise use and is not directed to children under 18 years of age.

## 13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via website notice, in-product notice, or by email when required.
