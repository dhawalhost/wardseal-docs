# Policy: Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

**Effective Date:** 15 March 2026

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between Wardseal ("Processor") and Customer ("Controller"), unless the parties otherwise agree in writing on role allocation.

## 1. Subject matter and duration

Processor will process Customer Personal Data to provide WardSeal services during the term of the agreement.

## 2. Nature and purpose of processing

Identity and access management processing, including authentication, tenant/user administration, policy/governance operations, and related logging/support activities.

## 3. Categories of data subjects

As determined by Customer, typically including employees, contractors, partners, and end users.

## 4. Categories of personal data

As determined by Customer, typically identity identifiers, authentication metadata, profile attributes, and audit/security event metadata.

## 5. Processor obligations

Processor will:

- Process data only on documented instructions from Controller
- Ensure personnel confidentiality obligations
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Assist Controller with data subject rights and compliance obligations
- Notify Controller of personal data breaches as required by law/contract
- Delete or return data at end of services, subject to legal retention obligations

## 6. Subprocessors

Controller authorizes use of subprocessors listed in `subprocessors-and-international-transfers.md`.

Processor will:

- Impose data protection obligations on subprocessors
- Remain responsible for subprocessors’ processing under this DPA
- Provide notice of material subprocessor changes where required

## 7. International transfers

Where required, transfers are governed by approved transfer mechanisms (for example SCCs) and supplementary safeguards.

For EU/EEA transfers, the parties will execute and apply applicable Standard Contractual Clauses (or lawful successor mechanisms) where needed.

For India-linked processing, parties will apply contractual and technical safeguards required under applicable Indian data protection law and implementing rules.

For U.S. state privacy compliance support, Processor will provide reasonable assistance to Controller for verified consumer rights requests and statutory assessments, to the extent relevant to services used.

## 8. Audit and information rights

Processor will make available reasonable information to demonstrate compliance and support audits subject to confidentiality, security, and scope limitations.

## 9. Security incidents

Processor will notify Controller of confirmed personal data breaches without undue delay, providing available information and updates.

## 10. Governing terms

Except as modified by this DPA, the agreement remains in effect. In case of conflict regarding data protection matters, this DPA prevails.
